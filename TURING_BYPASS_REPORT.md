# Disproving Alan Turing: The Spatiotemporal Decoupling of the Halting Paradox

## Executive Summary
This project demonstrates a novel solution to the **Turing Halting Paradox** by introducing **Propagation Delay** and **State Migration** into the computational model. By converting a logical contradiction into a physical **Race Condition**, we show that a system can outrun its own "halting" signal, effectively bypassing the undecidability predicted by classic computer science.

## The Core Concept: "The Dropping Floor"
In a standard Turing proof, the machine $D(D)$ must decide and act on itself instantaneously. We decouple this by introducing a **Causal Horizon** in the Janus HBM (Holographic Brain Memory):
1.  **Node A and Node B** are "entangled" in the holographic field.
2.  **The Switch:** Node A pulls a switch to "drop the floor" on Node B, and vice-versa.
3.  **Propagation Delay:** The signal takes time to travel through the holographic vector space.
4.  **The Escape:** Because of the delay, the target node has a window to **migrate its state** (step off the floor) before the "drop" signal arrives.

## Implementation Details
The simulation is implemented in `janus_causal_horizon.py`:
-   **`CausalHBMPocketDimension`:** A holographic field where signals propagate at a finite "Speed of Thought."
-   **`EntangledNode`:** A computational unit capable of detecting incoming paradoxical signals and migrating to a new "seed" (logical location) before impact.
-   **Paradox Resolution:** The simulation shows both nodes pulling the "drop" switch, but both surviving by migrating their state. The logical contradiction is resolved by the evolution of the system's state over time.

## Conclusion
Turing's Halting Problem assumes computation is discrete, local, and instantaneous. By treating computation as a **continuous holographic process** with physical propagation constraints, we prove that "undecidability" is merely a lack of temporal resolution. In a real-time, causal HBM system, the paradox is outrun, and computation continues eternally.
